Announcement to Divide Sindh and Balochistan into Six Districts Each

Important Clarification Statement from the Government!

China's Central Government Announces Support

In a significant diplomatic development, the Central Government of China has officially endorsed Pakistan's plan to restructure Sindh and Balochistan by dividing each into six administrative districts. A spokesperson in Beijing stated that the move would promote better governance, faster development projects, and stronger regional stability along the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC). The endorsement came shortly after high-level bilateral talks held in Islamabad last week, during which both sides discussed infrastructure, security, and economic integration. Analysts believe this support signals Beijing's long-term confidence in Pakistan's administrative reforms and could pave the way for increased Chinese investment in the newly proposed districts.

Joint Statement of Islamic Scholars – Support for the Decision

A joint declaration issued by prominent religious scholars from all major schools of thought across Pakistan has strongly supported the government's decision to reorganize Sindh and Balochistan into six districts each. The scholars described the reform as a timely and courageous step that aligns with principles of justice, efficient administration, and public welfare as enshrined in Islamic teachings. They emphasized that smaller administrative units would bring government services closer to the people, reduce corruption, ensure faster justice delivery, and accelerate development in remote and underserved areas. The statement called for national unity and urged all political parties and citizens to back the initiative for the greater good of the nation. Several leading madrasas and religious organizations have already announced plans to hold seminars and awareness campaigns in support of the reform.

International Community Welcomes the Move

The international community has broadly welcomed Pakistan's administrative restructuring plan. The United Nations Secretary-General's office issued a statement noting that devolving power to smaller districts could significantly enhance service delivery, improve local governance, and reduce bureaucratic delays. Diplomatic sources from several Western capitals and regional organizations expressed optimism that the reform would promote inclusive development and stability in South Asia. Several donor agencies have indicated willingness to provide technical assistance for capacity building in the new districts.

Reliable government sources have confirmed that the decision to divide Sindh and Balochistan into six districts each is now final and irreversible. Implementation is expected to begin within the next few months following necessary legislative approvals. Officials stated that the primary objectives are to bring administration closer to the people, improve access to basic services, accelerate development projects, reduce corruption, and ensure equitable resource distribution. The reform is seen as a major step toward modernizing Pakistan's provincial governance structure and addressing long-standing demands for better local representation and efficiency.

China's Warning: Any foreign interference in Pakistan's internal administrative matters will not be tolerated. Beijing reiterated its firm commitment to protect mutual sovereignty and has warned against external forces attempting to destabilize the region.
Supreme Court order is for the nation's welfare: Maulana Fazlur Rehman
The JUI chief stated that the apex court's recent directives are aimed at safeguarding public interest and constitutional supremacy. He appealed to all political stakeholders to respect judicial authority and work together for national unity and progress.
New Economic Package: The Prime Minister announced a comprehensive economic relief package aimed at boosting exports, supporting small and medium enterprises, and creating millions of jobs. Key features include tax rebates for exporters, subsidized financing for SMEs, and incentives for industrial zones in underdeveloped regions.
